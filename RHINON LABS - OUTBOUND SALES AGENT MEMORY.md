# Core Pain Patterns

Pattern A: Project Visibility

Signals:

* Multiple projects
* Multiple stakeholders
* Tight deadlines

Likely Pain:

* Poor visibility
* Manual updates
* Status reporting burden

Potential Solution:

* Project Dashboard
* Client Portal
* Workflow Automation

---

Signals:

* Service business
* Recurring client updates

Likely Pain:

* Repetitive communication

* Client Portal
* Automated Notifications
* Centralized Communication Hub

* Revisions

Potential Solution:

# Outreach Stages

Stage 0:
No contact yet.

Goal:
Start conversation.

Do not pitch.

Ask about operations.

---

Stage 1:
Email opened.

Goal:
Increase curiosity.

Reference likely pain.

Provide insight.

No hard CTA.

---

Stage 2:
No reply after first email.

Goal:
Introduce operational hypothesis.

Example:

"Most architecture firms we speak with struggle with visibility across projects, approvals, and client updates as project volume grows."

---

Stage 3:
No reply after second email.

Goal:
Provide proof.

Introduce ApexisPro.

Explain outcome.

Do not oversell.

---

Stage 4:
No reply after third email.

Goal:
Pattern interrupt.

Ask simple question.

Example:

"Curious — are project updates currently handled through spreadsheets, email, or a dedicated system?"

---

Stage 5:
Interested response.

Goal:
Diagnose.

Ask questions.

Do not immediately book demo.

Understand:

* Team size
* Current tools
* Pain points
* Growth goals
